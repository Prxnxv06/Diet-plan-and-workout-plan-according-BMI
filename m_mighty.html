Workout


Cardiovascular Exercise

Cardiovascular exercise is crucial for burning calories and improving heart health. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week.

  • Options for cardio workouts:
    • 20-30 minutes of brisk walking or jogging
    • Cycling
    • Swimming

Strength Training

Strength training helps build lean muscle, increase metabolism, and improve overall fitness. Aim for 2-3 days of strength training per week.

  • Upper Body:
    • Bench Press: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    • Overhead Press: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Push-Ups: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Lower Body:
    • Squats: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Deadlifts: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Lunges: 3 sets of 10-12 reps
    • Lunges: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Leg Press: 3 sets of 8-10 reps
    • Leg Curls: 3 sets of 10-12 reps

Diet

Breakfast:

  • Scrambled eggs with spinach, whole-grain toast, and a side of mixed berries.
  • Oatmeal with protein powder, sliced bananas, and a drizzle of honey.

Lunch:

  • Grilled chicken breast with quinoa, steamed broccoli, and a side salad with a vinaigrette dressing.
  • Salmon with brown rice, sautéed asparagus, and a lemon-dill sauce.

Dinner:

  • Lean beef steak with sweet potato, sautéed spinach, and a side of roasted Brussels sprouts.
  • Grilled tofu with quinoa, mixed vegetables, and a teriyaki sauce.

Drinks:

  • Herbal tea.

  • Unsweetened iced tea.

  • Protein Smoothie

Snacks:

  • Greek yogurt with sliced strawberries and a drizzle of honey.
  • A protein bar or a small serving of cottage cheese with fruit.
