Workout



Cardiovascular Exercise

Cardiovascular exercise is crucial for burning calories and improving heart health. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week.

  • Options for cardio workouts:
    • Brisk walking
    • Low-impact aerobics
    • Swimming
    • Cycling


Strength Training

Strength training helps build lean muscle, increase metabolism, and improve overall fitness. Aim for 2-3 days of strength training per week.

  • Bodyweight exercises:
    • Bodyweight squats
    • Push-ups (use a modified or elevated surface if needed)
    • Planks
  • Resistance band exercises:
    • Bicep curls
    • Seated rows
    • Leg lifts

Diet

Breakfast:

  • Greek yogurt with fresh berries and a drizzle of honey.

  • Oatmeal topped with sliced bananas and a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Lunch:

  • A side of whole-grain bread or brown rice.

  • Grilled chicken or tofu salad with mixed greens, cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, and a light vinaigrette dressing.

Dinner:

  • Baked or grilled salmon with a lemon-dill sauce.

  • Steamed broccoli and quinoa.

  • A mixed salad with olive oil and vinegar dressing.

Drinks:

  • Herbal tea.

  • Unsweetened iced tea.

Snacks:

  • Fresh Fruits.

  • Vegetable Sticks.

  • Greek Yogurt.
