Workout



Cardiovascular Exercise

Cardiovascular exercise is crucial for burning calories and improving heart health. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week.

  • Options for cardio workouts:
    • Brisk walking
    • Low-impact aerobics
    • Swimming
    • Cycling


Strength Training

Strength training helps build lean muscle, increase metabolism, and improve overall fitness. Aim for 2-3 days of strength training per week.

  • Bodyweight exercises:
    • Bodyweight squats
    • Push-ups (use a modified or elevated surface if needed)
    • Planks
  • Resistance band exercises:
    • Bicep curls
    • Seated rows
    • Leg lifts

Diet

Breakfast:

  • Oatmeal with protein powder, sliced bananas, and a drizzle of honey.
  • Greek yogurt parfait with granola, mixed berries, and a teaspoon of chia seeds.

Lunch:

  • Grilled chicken breast with quinoa, steamed broccoli, and a side salad with a vinaigrette dressing.
  • Mixed bean salad with chickpeas, black beans, vegetables, and a light vinaigrette dressing.

Dinner:

  • Baked salmon with asparagus, brown rice, and a lemon-dill sauce.
  • Grilled tofu with quinoa, mixed vegetables, and a light teriyaki sauce.

Drinks:

  • Herbal tea.

  • Unsweetened iced tea.

  • Protein smoothie

Snacks:

  • Greek yogurt with sliced strawberries and a drizzle of honey.
  • A protein bar or a small serving of low-fat cottage cheese with fruit.
